OverView of Lentils


Lentil is self-pollinating. Early maturing varieties flower at the 11th or 12th node stage and later maturing varieties at the 13th or 14th node stage.

Flowers appear in clusters of two or three at the base of the upper leaves, and flowering will be

delayed or flowers will abort in high moisture and high fertility conditions

    1)the lentil plant usually has two or more secondary branches rising from the main stem

    2)the majority of crop yield comes from branches from the uppermost nodes of the main stem, below the first flower node

    3)seed pods are less than an inch in length and contain one to two seeds

    4)seeds are lens-shaped with a range of seed coat colours as well as yellow, green or red cotyledons
