OverView of Peas


The common cultivated field pea is botanically classified as Pisum sativum, white flowered.

Another type, Austrian or maple pea , has purple flowers and is grown on a small acreage for special markets.

Alberta produces both long-vined indeterminate pea types (internodes 4 to 8 in. or 10 to 20 cm long) and short-vined determinate

types by 1 to 3 in. or 2.5 to 7.5 cm long.

    1)field pea is an annual herbaceous plant with slender succulent stems 1 to 6 ft. (.3 to 1.8 m) long

    2)the plant normally has a single stem, but has the ability to form tillers under certain stress conditions

        stresses that could cause tiller growth include early-season frost, hail, cutworm damage or moist conditions

        following a mid-season drought

    3)pea foliage colour is generally green to pale green

    4)two leaf structures characterize field pea leafed types produce a leaf consisting of one to three pairs of leaflets and

       simple branched terminal tendrils

    5)semi-leafless types have no leaflets on the leaf axil and compound tendrils that replace leaflets

    6)reproductive nodes bear either one, two or multiple flowers on peduncles that originate from the stem axis

        the flowers are highly self-pollinated

    7)the number of reproductive nodes produced on indeterminate types corresponds with the length of the flowering period

        which may be prolonged by cool, wet weather

    8)more determinate varieties tend to produce reproductive nodes in a shorter period, which results in earlier maturity these types

        can be severely affected by drought and heat stress during the reproductive period

    9)pea pods are normally 2 to 3 in. 5 to 7 cm long and contain four to nine seeds, depending on the variety and growing conditions

        crop stress can affect pod size and seed development

    10)seed shape varies by cultivar from round to angular or blocky, with a smooth or dimpled seed coat

    11)pea seed consists of the seed coat , the seed leaves and the embryo axis

            11.1)the seed coat encloses and protects the cotyledons and the embryo axis

            11.2)the embryo axis is comprised of a rudimentary root and a shoot
