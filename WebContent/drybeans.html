OverView of DryBeans


Dry bean cultivated types are herbaceous annuals, determinate or indeterminate in growth habit which bear flowers in axillary and terminal racemes.

Flowers are normally self fertilized, developing into a straight or slightly curved pod.

Seeds may be round, elliptical, somewhat flattened, or rounded-elongated in shape and have a rich assortment of coat colours and patterns.

Seeds of varieties produced in Alberta range in size from 170-400 gm per 1000 seeds.

    1)determinate types have a central main stem with five to nine nodes and from two or more branches that arise from the more basal nodes

    2)indeterminate types have a main stem with 12 to 15 nodes, or even more in climbing vine types

    3)germination is epigeal and requires approximately 7 days at a soil temperature of 16 degrees Centigrade

    4)days-to-flower varies with variety, temperature and photo period and can take up to 50 days
